Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 8, 2026

Overview

LidGuard is a macOS application for laptop theft protection. Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains what data the app accesses and how it is used.

Data Collection

LidGuard does not collect, store, or transmit any data to the developer or any third-party servers. The developer has no access to your data.

Data Accessed by the App

When theft mode is activated, LidGuard accesses the following data on your device:

  • Precise Location — GPS coordinates of your device
  • Wi-Fi Network — name of the connected network
  • IP Address — your device's public IP address
  • Battery Level — current battery percentage
  • Contacts — optionally, a selected contact's name and phone number for the lock screen message

Where Data Goes

All data is sent exclusively to your own Telegram bot that you configure. The data travels directly from your device to Telegram's servers using your personal bot token. The developer never receives, processes, or has access to any of this data.

Data Storage

LidGuard stores the following locally on your device:

  • App settings (UserDefaults)
  • Telegram bot token and chat ID (macOS Keychain, encrypted)
  • Activity log (local JSON file, max 500 entries)

No data is stored on external servers.

Third-Party Services

LidGuard communicates only with the Telegram Bot API using credentials you provide. No analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs are included.

Bluetooth

LidGuard uses Bluetooth to detect proximity of your paired devices for the auto-arm feature. Bluetooth data is processed locally and never transmitted.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date.
